Page Descriptions

Enhancements
The information on pages under this menu heading are restricted only to enhancement work. These are work items defined as up to 80 hours of IT effort. Charts within this menu display the breakdown of the backlog by various metrics. Sections of the chart are clickable, which will filter the backlog table by the specified metric.
Projects
Pages under this menu heading are restricted only to project-related work. Project grooming will take place after GPO approval, but before senior management approval. Active projects and their component stories are pulled real-time from Rally. As such, you may experience a slight delay in page load time.
Metrics
Team velocity, or total story points delivered per sprint, is the key metric of an agile team. This menu heading contains a consolidated list of all project and enhancement stories delivered or scheduled in a sprint as well as a graph of the team's historical velocity.
Refresh Story
Due to the large volume of data, enhancement and project grooming stories are stored in a local database that is refreshed from Rally once an hour. This form can be used to force synchronization of data for one specific story.

Status Definitions

Backlog
Request has been approved by the GPO, but not yet groomed.
Backlog/Ready 
The story has been prioritized to be groomed ASAP.
Defined
Grooming has been completed and is ready for delivery team review.
Defined/Ready 
Delivery team has reviewed and accepts the story. It is eligible to be scheduled in a future sprint.
In-Progress
Delivery team is actively working on implementing the story.
In-Progress/Ready 
Development is done and ready for IT functional testing.
Completed
Story is signed off by IT team. Enhancements have been deployed to UAT instance.
Completed/Ready 
UAT is complete and story is ready to go to production.
Accepted - Enhancements
Production deployment is complete, story is fully delivered.
Accepted - Project Delivery
Delivery lead has accepted work, resources can move on to the next story.

Pending UAT Legend

Very Late
Stories pending UAT for more than 28 days or two complete sprints are highlighted in this style.
Late
Stories pending UAT for 15 to 28 days between one and two complete sprints are highlighted in this style.
Ready for Testing
Stories pending UAT for not more than 14 days or one complete sprint are highlighted in this style.

Earned Value Definitions

Each response from the three categories defined below is given a specific numerical score from 1-5. The Earned Value (EV) is calculated by multiplying these three values together EV = J x R x V. This score should be used as guidance to prioritize grooming. If there is a compelling business reason to prioritize a story with a lower calculated earned value, this should be addresesd by the GPO steering committee.

Justification
Why are we doing this work?
  • Controls Improvement: Improvement of accounting controls for compliance.
  • Efficiency Improvement: Decrease waste.
  • Reduce Time to Close: Decrease waste specific to closing Red Hat books.
  • Regulatory Requirement: Legislation, Tax ruling, etc.
Reason
This response provides more details into the Justification and, in some cases, defines a success metric.
  • Controls Improvement: Is it an internal or external control that will be improved?
  • Efficiency Improvement: Will we save 0-5 hours/month, 5-10, 10-20 or 20+?
  • Reduce Time to Close: As above, but savings must be related to closing the financial books.
  • Regulatory Requirement: Free-form response indicating the specific requirement we must meet.
Value/Impact
This response provides information on what segment of Red Hat will be impacted.
  • Local Department: Impacts one local department within the greater EBE organization (eg. China Tax).
  • Regional Department: Impacts a department within EBE across all countries within a specific region (eg. LATAM Credit).
  • Global Department: Impacts a department within EBE across all regions (eg. GBD Project Accounting).
  • All of Red Hat: Potential impact to all associates, not isolated to EBE (eg. iExpenses).
  • Customer/Supplier: External impact to members of Red Hat's trading community (eg. Customer Statements).
